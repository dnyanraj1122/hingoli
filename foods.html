Foods

Khichdi is a simple, healthy, and traditional Indian dish made by cooking rice and lentils (dal) together. It is often considered a comfort food because it is easy to digest and full of nutrition. Sometimes vegetables like carrots, peas, or potatoes are added to make it more wholesome. Khichdi is usually served with ghee, curd, or pickle and is popular in many Indian households. It is often prepared during fasting, illness, or as a light, everyday meal. In some regions, khichdi also has cultural and religious significance, like during the Makar Sankranti festival, where it is offered as prasad. Known for its simplicity and health benefits, khichdi is often called the "soul food" of India.

Puran Poli is a traditional and popular sweet dish from Maharashtra, especially prepared during festivals like Gudi Padwa, Holi, and Diwali. It is a type of stuffed flatbread made with a filling called puran, which is a mixture of chana dal (split Bengal gram), jaggery, cardamom, and nutmeg. The outer covering, called poli, is made from wheat or all-purpose flour (maida). The dough is rolled out, filled with the sweet mixture, and then cooked on a hot griddle with a little ghee. Puran Poli is soft, flavorful, and enjoyed by people of all ages. It is usually served with ghee, milk, or katachi amti (a thin spicy dal). This dish represents the rich culinary heritage of Maharashtra and is a symbol of festivity and tradition.

Bhakri and Bhaji is a traditional and wholesome meal commonly eaten in rural and urban areas of Maharashtra. Bhakri is a type of flatbread made from jowar (sorghum), bajra (pearl millet), or rice flour, and is usually thicker than regular chapati. It is cooked on a hot griddle and sometimes puffed directly over the flame. Bhaji refers to a vegetable dish, which can be dry or with gravy, made using seasonal vegetables like brinjal (vangi), potato, methi, or spinach with traditional spices. This combination is often served with thecha (a spicy chutney made of green chilies and garlic) and raw onion, making it a simple yet flavorful and nutritious meal. Bhakri and Bhaji reflect the rustic charm and rich food culture of Maharashtra.
